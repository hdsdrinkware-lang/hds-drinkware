Introduction

For B2B buyers, Amazon FBA sellers, and e-commerce brand owners, the unit cost quoted by a Chinese manufacturer is only the first part of the pricing puzzle. To accurately calculate profit margins, determine retail prices, and plan inventory, you must know the true Landed Cost. Landed cost represents the total expense of a product from the factory assembly line to your warehouse floor. This guide provides a practical formula and a real-world importing case study.

The Landed Cost Formula

To calculate your landed cost per unit, use the following comprehensive formula:

Landed Cost per Unit = (Product Unit Cost + Shipping Cost + Customs Duties + Port & Clearence Fees + Local Logistics) / Total Units Ordered

Let's break down each component of this formula:

  • Product Unit Cost: The price per tumbler/bottle, including customization (logo, customized paint) and standard packaging.
  • Shipping Cost: The total sea freight, air freight, or express courier cost to move the goods from China to your destination country.
  • Customs Duties: Import tariffs and customs taxes based on the Harmonized System (HS) code of your drinkware in your country.
  • Port & Clearance Fees: Inland handling, customs broker fees, terminal handling charges, and examination costs.
  • Local Logistics: Final trucking or courier delivery fees from the arrival port or airport to your warehouse.

Trade terms

Understanding Shipping Terms (Incoterms)

Incoterm Seller Responsibility Buyer Responsibility Landed Cost Impact
EXW (Ex-Works) Manufactures the goods and packs them for pickup. Handles export clearance, global shipping, customs, and local logistics. Lowest manufacturer price, but buyer must manage and calculate all shipping components manually.
FOB (Free on Board) Delivers goods to China port and handles China customs clearance. Manages ocean freight, import clearance, duties, and local delivery. Standard trade term for larger volume buyers who have their own freight forwarders.
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) Handles manufacturing, ocean freight, customs clearance, duties, taxes, and final doorstep delivery. Receives the goods at their warehouse. Easiest option for Amazon FBA and Shopify sellers. The manufacturer quotes an all-inclusive DDP price, which equals your Landed Cost.

Case Study: Sourcing 1,000 Custom 40oz Tumblers to USA

Let's run a realistic calculation for a US-based Amazon seller ordering 1,000 custom 40oz handle tumblers under FOB and DDP terms:

Scenario A: Sourcing under DDP (All-Inclusive)

  • HDS quoted DDP Ocean Unit Cost: $4.50 per unit
  • Total units: 1,000
  • Total Cost to Warehouse: $4,500
  • Landed Cost per Unit: $4.50 (Extremely simple, no hidden fees)

Scenario B: Sourcing under FOB (Traditional)

  • Factory FOB Unit Cost: $2.80 per unit ($2,800 total)
  • Ocean Freight (LCL Shipping to LA Port): $850 total
  • Customs Duties (approx. 5.3% duty rate for stainless steel tumblers): $148.40
  • Customs Broker & Port Fees (ISF, Bond, HMF, MPF): $350 total
  • Local Trucking (from LA Port to Warehouse): $400 total
  • Total Cost: $2,800 + $850 + $148.40 + $350 + $400 = $4,548.40
  • Landed Cost per Unit: $4.55

In this case study, while the factory's raw product price was only $2.80 under FOB, the actual Landed Cost rose to $4.55 once all shipping and custom fees were accounted for. This highlights why comparing DDP vs FOB options is essential for sound B2B decision-making.

What is landed cost in B2B importing?

Landed cost is the total price of a product once it has arrived at the buyer's doorstep or warehouse. It includes the unit cost, sea/air shipping, customs duties, port fees, local trucking, and insurance.

What are the main shipping terms for custom drinkware?

The main terms are EXW (Ex-Works: buyer handles shipping), FOB (Free on Board: seller delivers to China port), and DDP (Delivered Duty Paid: seller handles everything including shipping and customs to buyer's door).

Why is DDP shipping popular for Amazon FBA sellers?

DDP is popular because the manufacturer or their freight forwarder manages customs clearance, pays all import duties and taxes, and delivers directly to Amazon warehouses, eliminating the need for sellers to manage logistics.
